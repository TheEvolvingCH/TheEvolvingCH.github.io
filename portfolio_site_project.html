Portfolio Site

A look into why I developed this site by writing every line of code

Created: November 14th, 2017

 1  The What:

My portfolio site, the source that likely drove you here.

 2  The How:

Once I decided I wanted to create a site I had to make the call on if I would start with a template or go forward on my own. A template would certainly be easier, faster and likely look more refined. But that's not something I necessarily wanted. I wanted to prove to myself, and to others, that I could overcome the challenge of developing it 100% on my own. If that meant that the site would be a bit gritty, so be it.

The first step was for me to write down the goals of what I wanted to accomplish with the site. Some of the main themes included establishing a web presence, conveying personality, communicating my professional background and highlighting my coding journey. Evaluating if a choice I was making helped me achieve these goals or not helped me navigate the project.

Next up was to mock up wireframes of the site layout. For this I turned to Adobe Comp and Adobe Sketch, my first time using both apps. Adobe Comp turned out to be a quick and easy way to visualize my ideas across devices.

With the general layouts defined, it was time to code. I took a mobile-first approach, working my way up to larger screen sizes.

 3  The Result:

Ultimately, I'll let you be the judge of that. From my perspective I hit on most of my initial goals. Would it have been cleaner built off of a template? Sure. Would it have been as rewarding? Hell no. Over the coming months you will see enhancements being made to grow & improve the site. I am always open to feedback, so feel free to contact me!

 4  Challenges:

Building this site from a blank slate has been the largest coding initiative I've taken to date and presented a myriad of challenges for me. When taking on a huge project like this it can be daunting to be at the starting point, looking up to the summit. I found that this process was a great opportunity to practice getting in the developer mindset. Breaking things down into smaller chunks, working through one piece at a time and testing along the way.

Not starting with a template also meant trying to create a visually pleasing design, while managing all other aspects of developing a site. This created some frustrations along the way. It was an exercise in remaining patient while learning new tools and getting things to a point where I was happy. Hopefully that effort is reflected in the site you see today.

Another major challenge has been working through cross-browser issues. All of the initial development testing was done in Chrome and all functionality, layouts, etc. worked great. It was a wakeup call to see how many issues can arise across browsers. I am still working through some of these but I have been able to address many of the major issues that occur.

Lastly, the content...I hate talking about myself. This is clearly an issue when trying to build a site around yourself. To address this I decided to write most of the content in the 3rd person, which made it a bit easier. While speaking this way any other time would be weird, it helped here. Don't judge.

Portfolio Site

A look into the creation of my page.

Updated: February 1st, 2018

 1  The What:

My portfolio site, the source that likely drove you here.

 2  The How:

When I initially built this site in 2017 I did so from the ground up, writing every line of code and testing across browsers. This process was a big step in my development path and I was very pleased with the outcome. However, after a few months I wanted to do an overhaul on the content. Around this time I was also getting familiar with Bootstrap. I saw this as an opportunity to work on my content while also getting hands on practice with the front-end toolkit. By rebuilding the site within Bootstrap, it would also allow me to quickly implement future updates without having to worry as much about cross-browser debugging.

Fortunately I took the time to define goals before the initial build of my site. The main themes of establishing a web presence, conveying personality, communicating my professional background and highlighting my coding journey remained true. Evaluating if a choice I was making helped me achieve these goals or not helped me navigate the project.

 3  The Result:

Ultimately, I'll let you be the judge of that. From my perspective I hit on most of my goals. I also took things a step further by implementing new features such as AJAX requests to highlight my ongoing professional development at Treehouse. As I continue to expand I will look to add other new elements.

I am always open to feedback, so feel free to contact me!

 4  Challenges:

One of the hardest things for me to do was to go through with moving away from my original site. I put extensive effort in to its development so the decision was not easy. However, for scaling the site moving forward I knew it was the right call.

Migrating to Bootstrap was not too difficult. Although it does take some time getting accustomed to using classes to define layout. I found the overall transition to be a good learning experience and look forward to growing the site within this framework.
